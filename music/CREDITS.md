# Music — background music for the game

Seven freely usable musical pieces evoke the mood of the steppe and the 5th century
(Huns, Magyars, Scythians, Turanian peoples): restrained, loopable ambient textures and
a few cheerful, melodic pieces — child-friendly, quiet underscoring.

> **A note on authenticity and the selection.** No sounding record of 5th-century Hun
> music has survived, so these pieces are not "period-accurate" but *evoke the mood* of
> the era. The selection focuses specifically on the Hun–Magyar–Scythian–Turanian sound
> world:
> • **Armenian duduk** (a close relative of the predecessor of the Hungarian tárogató),
> • **ney and kaval** (Turanian–Anatolian pipes, nomadic shepherd music),
> • **saz / bağlama** (Turkic-nomadic lute, a relative of the Hungarian koboz),
> • **dulcimer** (the ancestor of the cimbalom, a Central Asian steppe instrument),
> • **ceremonial ambient** and **choral, epic** pieces (Kevin MacLeod).
> We deliberately **avoided the Chinese/Japanese and "Arabian bazaar" clichés**.

Every piece is under a free license. Attribution of the CC-BY pieces is a **license
condition** — that is why it appears here and on the music-button label as well.
